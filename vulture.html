vulture

Facts


Vultures are large birds of prey that usually feed on carrion (dead or dying animals and rocks).
Vultures use their large wings to soar in the air for many miles without having to flap.
Vultures are also called buzzards.
Vultures from the Old World (Europe, Asia, and Africa) are not related to the vultures of the New World (North and South America).
They look quite similar, though. Both have heads and necks with no feathers.
Old World vultures are related to eagles and hawks and use sight to find their food.
New World vultures are related to storks and use their sense of smell to find their food.
Vultures symbolize death in literature.
