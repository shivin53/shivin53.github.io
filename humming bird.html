humming bird

Facts

Like a crown jewel, a hummingbird nest is one of the great wonders in all of nature.
They are so tiny, so perfect. Yet, few of us have ever seen a hummingbird nest.
This is because they are nearly impossible to find.
From the ground, they look like another bump on a branch.
From above, an umbrella of leaves conceals them.
And from the side, they look like a tiny knot, quilted with lichens, plant down and fibers.
Though each of the 17 hummingbird species that breed in North America builds slightly different nests in various habitats, all hummingbird nests have much in common.
Hummingbird eggs are about the size of navy beans.
Most females lay two eggs, which they incubate for 15 to 18 days.
